Tired of coding?
Why not start a boutique technical recruiting company in your city?

I’ve been coding for three years, liked it but did not love it, so I started a technical recruiting agency.

I find it a great way to give back to the tech-community and it helped me to earn a nice side-income.

I believe, software engineers who are “people-people” and are willing to learn some business and sales skills

will outperform established recruiting agencies and staffing firms at least by a factor of ten.

Here are five reasons why I think so:

As a coder, you will have more credibility, when talking to candidates

Many programmers hate recruiters because they often feel not understood by them. If you introduce yourself as a software engineer who became a recruiters, candidates are much more likely to listen to you. As a tech-person will have a much easier time to grok all the aspects why people want to change jobs.

As a coder you will have more credibility, when talking to clients.

Every business starts with clients. In recruiting, it is common that clients pay 15%-25% of the yearly salary of the candidate to the recruiter. That is a lot of money. However, firms are often upset to get low-quality candidates through that funnel from non-technical recruiters. (Before the dot-com crash, around year 1999, the headhunting-fee for software engineers was 100% of the yearly salary.) Thus, charging like normal agencies while delivering better quality will put you ahead of traditional recruiting agencies.

You understand tech.​

Need help turning leads into sales? We got you.

No matter whether you have been a Java, .Net, Javascript, Node, Python or Rube developer. In the end it’s all just if-else and for-loops. If you understand the details of one stack really well, you will be also able to assess other stacks. For instance, if you worked with Python a lot, you will also understand that a good Ruby firm, is only a good place to work if they have proper testing.

You know intuitively which people fit to which companies.

You know that neckbearded hipster, who is very experiment-friendly but maybe not so rigorous will not fit into Oracle-ish firm. As a programmer, you have been exposed to different nerd-types and you will have a much easier time to cluster candidates and find matching jobs for them.

You know the community.

Knowing the coder-community in Zurich kickstarted my recruiting business. I started going to tech-meetups wearing a black t-shirt that had “Looking for cool-tech jobs, TALK TO ME!” written in Terminal-Font on front and back which I designed in three minutes when waiting for the bus. This shirt-thing was the highest recruiting-related ROI activity I have ever done. The attention and deals I got through it were invaluable for the kickstart and growth of coderfit.
